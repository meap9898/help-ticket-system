1. The "Have You Tried Turning It Off and On Again?"

Its a cliche for a reason. A full Restart This clears the system's temporary memory (RAM) and kills hung background processes that might be blocking your app from launching.
2. Check the Task Manager

Sometimes an application is "open" but frozen in a ghost state where the window won't appear.

    Action: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc.

    Look for: The app name under the "Processes" tab.

    Test: If you see it, right-click and select End Task, then try opening it again.

3. Verify Network & VPN Connection

Many work applications (like Outlook, Teams, or internal databases) require a "handshake" with a server to open.

    Action: Check if your Wi-Fi/Ethernet is connected.

    The VPN Factor: If you are working remotely, ensure your VPN is active. Many enterprise apps won't even launch if they can't see the corporate network.

4. Check for Pending Updates

If Windows or macOS is mid-update, or if the specific app has a mandatory update waiting, it might refuse to launch.

    Action: Go to Settings > Update & Security and see if there is a "Restart Required" message.

5. Disk Space & Memory Stress

If your hard drive is 99% full, the computer lacks the "scratch space" needed to launch new programs.

    Action: Open "This PC" or "Finder" and check your local disk (C:) space. If it’s in the red, that’s your culprit.

What to Include in Your IT Ticket

If none of the above works, your IT team will love you if you provide these details upfront:
Detail	Why it matters
Exact Error Message	Helps IT find the specific "stop code" in their database.
Scope	Is it one app (e.g., just Excel) or all apps?
Timing	Did this start after a recent update or a power outage?
Self-Tests	Mention: "I already restarted and checked my VPN."
